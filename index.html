ABOUT

I'm currently a Junior Researcher at ISI Foundation, where I am working in the field of AI/NLP to build a dashboard combining (big) data on news media, parliamentary speeches, and digital traces. With this project — developed in partnership with the OECD — we aim at providing policymakers, journalists, and civil society organisations a mean to explore the public debate about ongoing complex (and likely divisive) policy projects.

Before coming to ISI, I was an Intern at the OECD Observatory of Public Sector Innovation (OPSI), where I contributed to the 2023 report on Global Trends and helped shape a project on algorithmic transparency. I was a consultant for the Digital Policy Alert (DPA), helping to build the biggest public, independent, and searchable dataset of tech policies. I was a Research Assistant for Michela Carlana (Harvard Kennedy School) and Eliana la Ferrara (Bocconi University) at LEAP.

EDUCATION

I graduated cum laude at Bocconi University (2023) and Sciences Po (2022), in the Double Degree Master's in Public Policy. That's a long name to say that I spent one year in Milan and one year in Paris, and I was formally a student of both universities. For Bocconi, I wrote a research thesis on algorithmic accountability in the public sector, under the supervision of Prof. Oreste Pollicino, where I studied the meaning of algorithmic accountability and explored how to ensure it in the public sector via policies.

In 2020, I received my Bachelor's degree cum laude in political science from the University of Bologna, with a thesis in microeconomics on positional goods (a fascinating topic, find out more here). During my BA, I had the opportunity to spend one semester at the University of Utrecht supported by an Erasmus+ scholarship.
