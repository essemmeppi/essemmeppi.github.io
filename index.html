ABOUT

I'm currently a Consultant at the OECD, where I work with EU governments to prototype LLM-powered digital services for civic participation and study global practices of AI use in the public sector. Before this, I co-authored the last two editions of the OECD Global Trends in Government Innovation (2023, 2024).

Previously, I was a Fellow at the ISI Foundation, where I worked with physicists of complex systems and policy analysts to build an NLP tool that helps governments assess the public acceptability of policy reforms. By analyzing digital traces — from online news, parliamentary speeches, and social media — our solution offers real-time insight into complex and often divisive policy debates. The project was developed in partnership with the OECD.

Earlier, I was an Intern at the OECD Observatory of Public Sector Innovation (OPSI). I was a consultant for the Digital Policy Alert (DPA), helping to build the biggest public, independent, and searchable dataset of tech policies. I was a Research Assistant for Michela Carlana (Harvard Kennedy School) and Eliana la Ferrara (Bocconi University) at LEAP.

EDUCATION

I graduated cum laude at Bocconi University (2023) and Sciences Po (2022), in the Double Degree Master's in Public Policy. That's a long name to say that I spent one year in Milan and one year in Paris, and I was formally a student of both universities. For Bocconi, I wrote a research thesis on algorithmic accountability in the public sector, under the supervision of Prof. Oreste Pollicino, where I studied the meaning of algorithmic accountability and explored how to ensure it in the public sector via policies. I was invited to present my thesis at Data for Policy 2024.

In 2020, I received my Bachelor's degree cum laude in political science from the University of Bologna, with a thesis in microeconomics on positional goods (a fascinating topic, find out more here). During my BA, I had the opportunity to spend one semester at the University of Utrecht supported by an Erasmus+ scholarship.
